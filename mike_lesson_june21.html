Review — Location & Small Talk

5 min warm-up
Teacher: Mike handled these well last week. Read each aloud; Mike translates and then answers naturally about his own home. Pay special attention to the встреч- family — Mike used all three forms in chat.
#EnglishRussianPronunciation
R1 Where is your office located? Где находится ваш офис? Gdye na-HOH-dee-tsa vash OH-fees?
R2 In what part of the city? В какой части города? V ka-KOY CHAS-tee GOH-ra-da?
R3 Flowers grow on my balcony. У меня на балконе растут цветы. Oo mee-NYA na bal-KOH-nye ras-TOOT tsvee-TY.
R4 I prefer to meet friends at my place. Я предпочитаю встречать друзей у себя дома. Ya preet-pa-chee-TA-yu fstree-CHAT' droo-ZYEY oo see-BYA DOH-ma.
R5 I often meet the neighbours and their dog. Я часто встречаю соседей и их собаку. Ya CHAS-tuh fstree-CHA-yu sa-SYE-dyey ee eekh sa-BA-koo.
R6 Tomorrow I will meet a friend. Завтра я встречу друга. ZAHF-truh ya FSTRYE-choo DROO-ga.
The встреч- family — three forms, three jobs: встреча (FSTRYE-cha) = a meeting (the noun) · встречаю (fstree-CHA-yu) = "I meet" (imperfective — habit, repeated) · встречу (FSTRYE-choo) = "I will meet" (perfective — one specific time). Same root, but the ending tells you whether it's a thing, a habit, or a one-time future event.
Bridge to today: Last week you asked в какой части города? — notice какой ("which / what kind") already changes its ending to match часть. That's adjective agreement, and today we make it a system.

Прилагательные — How Adjectives Agree

14 min
Teacher: An adjective in Russian must match its noun in gender and number. Mike's French and German already trained this instinct — Russian just has its own set of endings. Show the four forms of one adjective, then drill. (QuickStudy p. 4, Adjectives.)

One Adjective, Four Endings — красивый (beautiful)

Goes with…EndingFormExample
Masculine -ый / -ий красивый kra-SEE-vyy красивый сад — a beautiful garden
Feminine -ая / -яя красивая kra-SEE-va-ya красивая книга — a beautiful book
Neuter -ое / -ee красивое kra-SEE-va-ye красивое озеро — a beautiful lake
Plural (all genders) -ые / -ие красивые kra-SEE-vy-ye красивые цветы — beautiful flowers
The trick Mike already knows: first find the noun's gender (the same question as with мой / моя / моё / мои), then give the adjective the matching ending. Possessives and adjectives follow the very same logic.
#EnglishRussianPronunciation
1 I live in a tall building. Я живу в высоком доме. Ya zhy-VOO v vy-SOH-kum DOH-mye.
2 It's an interesting book. Это интересная книга. EH-ta een-tee-RYES-na-ya KNEE-ga.
3 Toronto is a big city. Торонто — большой город. Ta-RON-tuh — bal'-SHOY GOH-rut.
4 We have a small but cosy apartment. У нас маленькая, но уютная квартира. Oo nas MA-leen'-ka-ya, no oo-YUT-na-ya kvar-TEE-ra.

Мини-диалог — Describing Home & City

role-play
Teacher: Pulls together adjectives, colours, and describing places. Mike is addressed with вы. Read once, then swap roles and let Mike describe his real apartment and view.
Олекс:
Майк, в какой части города вы живёте?
Mayk, v ka-KOY CHAS-tee GOH-ra-da vy zhy-VYO-tye?
Mike, what part of the city do you live in?
Майк:
Мы живём в центре, в высокой современной многоэтажке.
My zhy-VYOM v TSEN-trye, v vy-SOH-kuy sa-vree-MYEN-nuy mna-ga-e-TASH-kye.
We live downtown, in a tall modern high-rise.
Олекс:
Красиво! Какой у вас вид?
Kra-SEE-vuh! Ka-KOY oo vas veed?
Lovely! What's your view like?
Майк:
У нас красивый вид на город. На балконе красные и жёлтые цветы.
Oo nas kra-SEE-vyy veed na GOH-rut. Na bal-KOH-nye KRAS-ny-ye ee ZHOL-ty-ye tsvee-TY.
We have a beautiful view of the city. On the balcony there are red and yellow flowers.
Олекс:
А какой ваш любимый цвет?
A ka-KOY vash lyu-BEE-myy tsvyet?

Майк:
Синий. У меня синий зонт и синяя машина.
SEE-niy. Oo mee-NYA SEE-niy zont ee SEE-nya-ya ma-SHY-na.
Blue. I have a blue umbrella and a blue car.
Олекс:
Отлично! Вы прекрасно описываете.
At-LEECH-nuh! Vy pree-KRAS-nuh a-PEE-sy-va-ye-tye.
Excellent! You describe things wonderfully.

📚 Домашнее задание — Homework for Mike

  1. Take one adjective (красивый, большой, новый) and write it in all four forms with a noun: masculine, feminine, neuter, plural.
  2. Describe 5 things in your apartment with a colour: чёрный ошейник, синий зонт, красные цветы… Match the colour ending to the noun.
  3. Describe your city in 3 sentences. Use: большой, красивый, современный, центр, вид.
  4. Answer in Russian: Какой ваш любимый цвет? Почему? ("What's your favourite colour? Why?")
  5. Translate without looking: "We live in a tall modern building and have a beautiful view of the city."
