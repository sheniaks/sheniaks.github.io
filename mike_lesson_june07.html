Two Ways to Say "In a Language"

по-русски
pa-ROOS-kee
"in Russian" — as an adverb, with verbs like speak, read, write, understand. Я читаю по-русски.
на русском (языке)
na ROOS-kum
"in Russian" — with a noun/object. Книга на русском. (A book in Russian.)
Rule of thumb: по- + language describes how you do something (говорить по-русски). на + language describes what something is in (фильм на русском). Mike's French/German give him a feel for this — both have similar splits.

Книги и чтение — Books & Reading

12 min
Teacher: Last time we named the parts of a book. Today Mike learns to have opinions about books — to describe, recommend, and react. This is where reading vocabulary becomes conversation.
Чтение — это моё любимое занятие.
CHTYE-nee-ye — EH-ta ma-YO lyu-BEE-ma-ye za-NYA-tee-ye.
Reading is my favourite pastime.
#EnglishRussianPronunciation
7 I'm reading an interesting book right now. Сейчас я читаю интересную книгу. See-CHAS ya chee-TA-yu een-tee-RYES-noo-yu KNEE-goo.
8 This book is about history. Эта книга об истории. EH-ta KNEE-ga ab ees-TOH-ree-ee.
9 I really like this author. Мне очень нравится этот автор. Mnye OH-chen' NRA-vee-tsa EH-tut AF-tur.
10 I can recommend this book to you. Я могу порекомендовать вам эту книгу. Ya ma-GOO pa-ree-ka-meen-da-VAT' vam EH-too KNEE-goo.
11 I prefer to read in the evening. Я предпочитаю читать вечером. Ya preet-pa-chee-TA-yu chee-TAT' VYE-chee-rum.
12 I want to read this novel in Russian someday. Когда-нибудь я хочу прочитать этот роман на русском. Kag-DA-nee-bood' ya ha-CHOO pra-chee-TAT' EH-tut ra-MAN na ROOS-kum.

Opinion Phrases — Saying What You Like

Мне нравится…
mnye NRA-vee-tsa
I like… (lit. "to me it is pleasing")
Я люблю…
ya lyub-LYU
I love… (stronger, for lasting passions)
Я предпочитаю…
ya preet-pa-chee-TA-yu
I prefer…
Мне интересно…
mnye een-tee-RYES-nuh
I find it interesting…
Careful with нравится: the thing you like is the subject, and "me" is in the dative (мне). Мне нравится книга literally = "the book is pleasing to me." If it's plural: мне нравятся книги.

Прогулки с Мабо — Narrating a Walk

10 min
Teacher: Mike already knows the commands (Сидеть, Ко мне, Пошли). Today we go beyond commands — full sentences that narrate a walk: what Mabo does, what Mike sees, how it goes. Conversational, not imperative.
#EnglishRussianPronunciation
13 Every morning I walk with Mabo. Каждое утро я гуляю с Мабо. KAZH-da-ye OOT-ruh ya goo-LYA-yu s MA-bo.
14 Mabo loves running across the grass. Мабо любит бегать по траве. MA-bo LYU-beet BYE-gat' pa tra-VYE.
15 He sniffs every tree. Он нюхает каждое дерево. On NYU-ha-yet KAZH-da-ye DYE-ree-vuh.
16 Sometimes he meets other dogs. Иногда он встречает других собак. Ee-nag-DA on fstree-CHA-yet droo-GEEKH sa-BAK.
17 When he gets tired, we go home. Когда он устаёт, мы идём домой. Kag-DA on oos-ta-YOT, my ee-DYOM da-MOY.
18 After the walk he sleeps all day. После прогулки он спит весь день. POS-lye pra-GOOL-kee on speet vyes' dyen'.
New verbs, all -ать type: бегать (to run around), нюхать (to sniff), встречать (to meet). They all follow the same easy present-tense pattern Mike knows from гулять → гуляю: бегает, нюхает, встречает. Regular and predictable.

📚 Домашнее задание — Homework for Mike

  1. Write 4 sentences about the languages you speak. Use both говорю по-… and на … языке. Compare two with труднее, чем…
  2. Describe a book you love in 3 sentences: what it's about (книга о…), the author (автор…), and your opinion (мне нравится / я люблю…).
  3. Narrate one of your walks with Mabo — 4 sentences using the new verbs бегает, нюхает, встречает, устаёт.
  4. Practise Мне нравится… with five different things (a book, a language, a city, the weather, Mabo). Remember: the thing is the subject!
  5. Translate without looking: "I'm reading a book in English, but someday I want to read in Russian."
