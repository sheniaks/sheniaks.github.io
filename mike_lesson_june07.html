Review — Motion Verbs in Action

5 min warm-up
Teacher: Mike used all of these last week — the motion verbs really clicked. Read each aloud; Mike translates. Pay attention to хожу / езжу / иду / еду and which one fits.
#EnglishRussianPronunciation
R1 I go to school every day. (on foot, habit) Я хожу в школу каждый день. Ya ha-ZHOO v SHKOH-loo KAZH-dyy dyen'.
R2 I go to work by car. (vehicle, habit) Я езжу на работу на машине. Ya YEZ-zhoo na ra-BOH-too na ma-SHY-nye.
R3 Today I went to a friend's place. (vehicle, one trip, past) Сегодня я поехал к другу. See-VOHD-nyuh ya pa-YE-hal k DROO-goo.
R4 In Winnipeg it's very hot and sunny — plus thirty. В Виннипеге очень жарко и солнечно — плюс тридцать. V Vee-nee-PEH-gye OH-chen' ZHAR-kuh ee SOHL-neech-nuh — plyus TREED-tsut'.
Did you notice? Every destination last week took a special ending: в школу, на работу, к другу. Those endings aren't random — they're the accusative and dative cases. Today we crack the first one open.

The Accusative Case — Винительный падеж

14 min
Teacher: The accusative answers two questions: "whom/what?" (the direct object) and "where to?" (the destination, with в/на). Don't overload Mike — focus on the two patterns he already uses every day. (QuickStudy p. 2–3, Accusative.)

What the Accusative Does

Two everyday jobs:

1. The direct object — the thing receiving the action: Я читаю книгу. (I read a book.)
2. The destination — where you're going, after в / на: Я иду в школу. (I'm going to school.)
Feminine nouns: -а → -у, -я → -ю
школа → в школу
работа → на работу
книга → книгу
среда → в среду
Masculine & neuter inanimate: no change!
парк → в парк
магазин → в магазин
кафе → в кафе
окно → в окно
The big relief for Mike: For inanimate masculine and neuter nouns, the accusative looks exactly like the dictionary form — парк → в парк, no change at all! Only feminine -а/-я words shift (to -у/-ю). That's why в парк was easy but в школу changed.
#EnglishRussianPronunciation
1 I'm going to the park on foot. Я иду ногами в парк. Ya ee-DOO na-GA-mee v park.
2 I'm going to the park by bus. Я еду на автобусе в парк. Ya YE-doo na af-TOH-boo-sye v park.
3 I go to the café on the corner of my house. Я хожу в кафе на углу моего дома. Ya ha-ZHOO v ka-FE na oog-LOO ma-ye-VO DOH-ma.
4 I go to the bookstore. Я хожу в книжный магазин. Ya ha-ZHOO v KNEEZH-nyy ma-ga-ZEEN.
5 I go to buy groceries. Я хожу покупать продукты. Ya ha-ZHOO pa-koo-PAT' pra-DOOK-ty.

Мой распорядок недели — My Weekly Routine

10 min
Teacher: Last week Mike used по + days beautifully — even ahead of the lesson. Let's make it explicit. В + day = one specific day ("on Monday"). По + plural day = something recurring ("on Mondays, every Monday"). This is Mike's actual weekly schedule.

В vs ПО — Two Ways to Say "On" a Day

В + accusative — one time
в понедельник=on Monday
во вторник=on Tuesday
в воскресенье=on Sunday
По + dative plural — recurring
по средам=on Wednesdays
по четвергам=on Thursdays
по субботам=on Saturdays

Mike's Real Week — Майк рассказывает о своей неделе

DayRussianEnglish
Понедельник В понедельник я готовлю план урока.
v puh-nee-DYEL'-neek ya ga-TOV-lyu plan oo-ROH-ka		 On Monday I prepare the lesson plan.
Вторник Во вторник Аня проверяет мой урок.
va FTOR-neek A-nya pra-vee-RYA-yet moy oo-ROK		 On Tuesday Anya checks my lesson.
По средам По средам я провожу урок английского.
pa SRYE-dam ya pra-va-ZHOO oo-ROK an-GLEE-skuh-vuh		 On Wednesdays I teach an English lesson.
По четвергам По четвергам я получаю фидбек от студента.
pa chet-veer-GAM ya pa-loo-CHA-yu FEED-bek at stoo-DYEN-ta		 On Thursdays I get feedback from a student.
По пятницам По пятницам я готовлюсь к уроку русского.
pa PYAT-nee-tsam ya ga-TOV-lyus' k oo-ROH-koo ROOS-kuh-vuh		 On Fridays I prepare for the Russian lesson.
По субботам По субботам я смотрю ютуб на русском.
pa soo-BOH-tam ya sma-TRYU yu-TOOB na ROOS-kum		 On Saturdays I watch YouTube in Russian.
Воскресенье В воскресенье у меня урок русского с Олексом.
v vus-kree-SYEN'-ye oo mee-NYA oo-ROK ROOS-kuh-vuh s a-LEK-sum		 On Sunday I have a Russian lesson with Oleks.
Spelling note: "во вторник" gets an extra -о (во, not в) because вторник starts with two consonants (вт-) — Russian adds the vowel to make it easier to say. Same reason we say во вторник but в среду.

Семьи слов — Word Families & Roots

8 min
Teacher: Mike has a great ear for these — he spotted them himself. Russian builds huge families from a single root. Recognising the root unlocks dozens of words. Here are the ones from last week, plus how they connect.
Root -крес-/-воскрес- — rising, resurrection
воскресеньеSunday vus-kree-SYEN'-ye
воскресrose / resurrected vus-KRYES
воскрешатьto resurrect vus-kree-SHAT'
Христос воскрес"Christ is risen" hrees-TOS vus-KRYES
Look-alikes: глава / голова
главаchapter; head (of org.) gla-VA
головаhead (body part) guh-la-VA
Same ancient root (head → leader → chapter). Глава государства = head of state. Болит голова = my head hurts.
Root верх- — top, upper
верхtop vyerkh
верхнийupper (m.) VYERH-niy
верхняяupper (f.) VYERH-nya-ya
наверхуup above na-veer-HOO
From last month: -зонт-/-зонд-
горизонтhorizon guh-ree-ZONT
зонтumbrella zont
зонтикlittle umbrella ZON-teek
зондprobe zond
The takeaway: When Mike meets a new word, the move is to ask "what root do I recognise?" верхняя полка (upper shelf) is instantly clear once he knows верх = top. His language-learner instinct is exactly right here.

Мини-диалог — Talking About the Week

role-play
Teacher: This pulls together the accusative (destinations), the routine pattern, and motion verbs. Mike is always addressed with вы. Read once, then swap roles.
Олекс:

Майк:
Сегодня прекрасный день — солнечно и тепло.
See-VOHD-nyuh pree-KRAS-nyy dyen' — SOHL-neech-nuh ee tep-LOH.
Today is a beautiful day — sunny and warm.
Олекс:

Pa SRYE-dam ya pra-va-ZHOO oo-ROK an-GLEE-skuh-vuh ya-zy-KA.
On Wednesdays I teach an English lesson.
Олекс:

A koo-DA vy ee-DYO-tye see-CHAS?

Отлично! А в воскресенье у нас урок русского.

Да! Я уже готовлюсь.

📚 Домашнее задание — Homework for Mike

  1. Write your own weekly routine — one sentence per day. Use в + day for one-offs and по + days for things you do regularly.
  2. Take five feminine nouns ending in -а (книга, школа, работа, машина, газета) and put each into the accusative after Я вижу… ("I see…"): Я вижу книгу.
  3. Say three destinations out loud: one on foot (иду в…), one by vehicle (еду в… на машине), one habitual (хожу в…).
  4. Word-family hunt: find one more word built on each root — верх-, глав-/голов-, воскрес-. Bring them Sunday.
  5. Translate without looking: "On Wednesdays I teach English, and on Sundays I have a Russian lesson."
