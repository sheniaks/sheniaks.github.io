The Iotated Vowel System — е, ё, я, ю

12 min
Teacher: Show this grid first. Each "iotated" vowel = a "y" sound + its hard partner. They are not new sounds — they are combinations Mike already knows from English.
а→я
"ah" → "yah"
like "ya" in yard
у→ю
"oo" → "yoo"
like "you" or Yule
э→е
"eh" → "yeh"
like "ye" in yes
о→ё
"oh" → "yoh"
like "yo" in yoga
Three Rules of Iotated Vowels:
1. At the start of a word — pure "y + vowel" sound: я, юг, ель, ёлка
2. After another vowel — same: моя (ma-YA), моё (ma-YOH)
3. After a consonant — the "y" disappears, the consonant is softened instead: тётя (TYO-tya) — the т is softened, no separate "y" sound.
Е е
"yeh" — the most common iotated vowel
Sounds like "ye" in yes
Trick for Mike: Whenever you see е, say "ye" as in yes. When unstressed, it reduces to a softer "ee" sound: неделя sounds like nee-DYEL-ya.

Two key rules:
1. Ё is always stressed. If you see ё, that syllable is the loudest.
2. After ш, ж, ч, щ the "y" disappears — ё just sounds like "o". That's why шёл = shol, not "shyol."

The Sound of Ы — Russia's Unique Vowel

10 min
Teacher: This is the only Russian vowel with no English equivalent. Spend real time here. Have Mike feel the position of his tongue.
Ы ы
"ɨ" — the murky vowel
No English equivalent. Made deep in the back of the mouth.
How to make the sound — three steps:
1. Smile slightly and start saying "ee" (as in see).
2. Without changing your lips, pull your tongue back in your mouth — like saying "oo" but with the lips of "ee."
3. The result is murky, like the "i" in ill said with clenched teeth, or the "u" in Russian but.

English approximation: The vowel in roses (the second one), or the "i" in bit said with the tongue pulled way back.

Putting It Together — Real Sentences

10 min
Teacher: Each sentence below is loaded with the sounds we drilled. After Mike reads each one, underline the iotated vowels and circle every ы. Have him read it again, paying attention to those marks.
#EnglishRussianPronunciation
1 I love my family very much. Я очень люблю свою семью. Ya OH-chen' lyub-LYU svah-YU seem-YU.
2 On the weekend we were resting at home. В выходные мы отдыхали дома. V vy-had-NY-yeh my at-dy-HA-lee DOH-ma.
3 Yesterday he walked to the store. Вчера он шёл в магазин. Vchee-RA on shol v ma-ga-ZIN.
4 Do you still want some tea? Ты ещё хочешь чай? Ty yee-SHCHO HOH-chesh' chai?
5 My aunt lives in the south. Моя тётя живёт на юге. Mah-YA TYO-tya zhi-VYOT na YU-gye.
6 The cake was delicious — give me one more piece. Пирог был восхитительный — дай мне ещё кусок. Pee-ROK byl vas-hee-TEE-teel'-nyy — day mnye yee-SHCHO koo-SOK.
7 We were learning Russian and we managed to clean up. Мы учили русский и успели убраться. My oo-CHEE-lee ROOS-kiy ee oos-PYE-lee oo-BRA-tsa.
8 The sun is shining and everything is light. Солнце светит и всё светло. SOHN-tseh SVYE-teet ee fsyo svet-LO.
Did you notice? Sentence 6 contains all four words from last week's struggle list: был (ы), восхитительный, ещё, and the rhythm of пирог. Sentence 3 has шёл. If Mike can read sentences 3 and 6 cleanly, today is a victory.
