Глаголы движения — Verbs of Motion

14 min
Teacher: This is one of Russian's trickiest topics, so go slowly. The key split: on foot vs by vehicle, and within "on foot," one direction now vs repeatedly / generally. Don't aim for mastery today — aim for the concept clicking. (QuickStudy p. 5, Verbs of Motion.)

The Big Three — On Foot vs By Vehicle

идти
eed-TEE
to be going (on foot), right now, in one direction
FOOT · ONE TRIP
ходить
hah-DEET'
to go (on foot) regularly, repeatedly, there & back
FOOT · HABIT
ехать
YE-hat'
to go by vehicle (car, bus, train, plane)
VEHICLE
The golden rule: If your feet touch the ground the whole way → идти / ходить. If you sit in something that moves → ехать. Even if you "go" to Toronto, you say ехать (or fly: лететь) because you're not walking there!

идти vs ходить — One Trip vs A Habit

#EnglishRussianPronunciation
1 I am going to the park now. (one trip, happening now) Я иду в парк сейчас. Ya ee-DOO v park see-CHAS.
2 I go to the park every day. (habit, repeated) Я хожу в парк каждый день. Ya ha-ZHOO v park KAZH-dyy dyen'.
3 Mabo and I are walking home. (one trip, now) Мы с Мабо идём домой. My s MA-bo ee-DYOM da-MOY.
4 We are driving to Toronto on Friday. (by vehicle) В пятницу мы едем в Торонто. V PYAT-nee-tsoo my YE-deem v ta-RON-tuh.
5 I often fly to Toronto. (habit, by air) Я часто летаю в Торонто. Ya CHAS-tuh lee-TA-yu v ta-RON-tuh.
Mike already knows this verb! Remember Идёт снег ("it's snowing," lit. "snow goes") and Пошли! ("let's go")? Both come from идти. You've been using motion verbs since lesson one.

Дни недели — Days of the Week, Revisited

8 min
Teacher: Mike has seen the days before. Today we add the crucial pattern: how to say "on" a day. Russian uses в + accusative. Point out that the days are not capitalised.
понедельник puh-nee-DYEL'-neek Monday
вторник FTOR-neek Tuesday
среда sree-DA Wednesday
четверг chet-VYERK Thursday
пятница PYAT-nee-tsa Friday
суббота soo-BOH-ta Saturday
воскресенье vus-kree-SYEN'-ye Sunday

Saying "On" a Day — в + Accusative

#EnglishRussianPronunciation
6 On Monday I study Russian. В понедельник я учу русский. V puh-nee-DYEL'-neek ya oo-CHOO ROOS-kiy.
7 On Wednesday we go to the library. В среду мы ходим в библиотеку. V SRYE-doo my HOH-deem v beeb-lee-a-TYE-koo.
8 On Sunday we have a lesson. В воскресенье у нас урок. V vus-kree-SYEN'-ye oo nas oo-ROK.
Watch the spelling shift: среда → в среду, пятница → в пятницу. The feminine -а ending becomes -у in the accusative. The other days don't change.

#EnglishRussianPronunciation
9 I love reading and learning languages. Я люблю читать и изучать языки. Ya lyub-LYU chee-TAT' ee ee-zoo-CHAT' ya-zy-KEE.
10 I have a large library at home. У меня дома большая библиотека. Oo mee-NYA DOH-ma bal'-SHA-ya beeb-lee-a-TYE-ka.
11 How many languages do you speak? Сколько языков вы знаете? SKOL'-ka ya-zy-KOF vy ZNA-ye-tye?
12 I am reading a novel in Russian. Я читаю роман на русском. Ya chee-TA-yu ra-MAN na ROOS-kum.
13 This book is on the top shelf. Эта книга на верхней полке. EH-ta KNEE-ga na VYERH-ney POL-kye.
14 I need a good dictionary. Мне нужен хороший словарь. Mnye NOO-zhen hah-ROH-shyy sla-VAR'.
15 What are you reading now? Что вы сейчас читаете? Shto vy see-CHAS chee-TA-ye-tye?
Two verbs for "study/read": читать = to read (text, books). изучать = to study / learn deeply (a subject, a language). учить = to learn / memorise. Mike изучает русский (studies it deeply) and учит новые слова (memorises new words).

Мини-диалог — In the Library

role-play
Teacher: This dialogue pulls together motion verbs, a day of the week, and Mike's book vocabulary. Read once, then swap roles.
Олекс:
Майк, куда вы идёте?
Mayk, koo-DA vy ee-DYO-tye?
Mike, where are you going?
Майк:
Я иду в библиотеку. Я хожу туда каждую среду.
Ya ee-DOO v beeb-lee-a-TYE-koo. Ya ha-ZHOO too-DA KAZH-doo-yu SRYE-doo.
I'm going to the library. I go there every Wednesday.
Олекс:
Что вы читаете сейчас?
Shto vy chee-TA-ye-tye see-CHAS?
What are you reading now?
Майк:
Я читаю роман на русском. Это трудно, но интересно.
Ya chee-TA-yu ra-MAN na ROOS-kum. EH-ta TROOD-nuh, no een-tee-RYES-nuh.
I'm reading a novel in Russian. It's hard, but interesting.
Олекс:
Сколько языков вы знаете?
SKOL'-ka ya-zy-KOF vy ZNA-ye-tye?
How many languages do you know?
Майк:
Английский, французский, немецкий, и теперь я изучаю русский.
An-GLEE-skiy, fran-TSOOZ-skiy, nee-MYETS-kiy, ee tee-PYER' ya ee-zoo-CHA-yu ROOS-kiy.
English, French, German, and now I'm learning Russian.
Олекс:
Молодец! У вас дома большая библиотека?
Muh-lah-DYETS! Oo vas DOH-ma bal'-SHA-ya beeb-lee-a-TYE-ka?
Well done! Do you have a big library at home?
Майк:
Да, очень большая. Я люблю книги.
Da, OH-chen' bal'-SHA-ya. Ya lyub-LYU KNEE-gee.
Yes, very big. I love books.

📚 Домашнее задание — Homework for Mike

  1. Write 3 sentences using motion verbs: one with иду (going now), one with хожу (a habit), one with еду (by vehicle).
  2. For each day of the week, write one short sentence: В понедельник я…, во вторник я… Use the "в + day" pattern.
  3. Describe your library in Russian — 3 sentences. Use: книга, полка, словарь, язык, читать, изучать.
  4. Translate without looking: "On Wednesday I go to the library and read a novel in Russian." Then check sentences 7 & 12.
  5. Pick five books off your shelf and say each author aloud: Это книга, автор — …
