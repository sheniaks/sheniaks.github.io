Погода и природа — Weather & Nature

10 min
Context: It's late April in Winnipeg — still cold, maybe patchy snow. These phrases connect to Mike's daily life and build on выпал снег from last week.
#EnglishRussianPronunciation

2 It's still cold outside, but the sun is already shining. На улице ещё холодно, но солнце уже светит. Na OO-lee-tseh yee-SHCHOH HOH-lud-nuh, no SOHN-tseh oo-ZHEH SVYEH-teet.
3 Yesterday it snowed, but today it is sunny. Вчера шёл снег, а сегодня солнечно. Vchee-RAH shyol snyek, a see-VOHD-nyuh SOHL-neech-nuh.
4 What is the weather like tomorrow? Какая завтра погода? Kah-KAH-yah ZAHF-truh pah-GOH-duh?
5 They say it will be warmer on Wednesday. Говорят, в среду будет теплее. Guh-vah-RYAT, v SRYEH-doo BOO-deet tep-LYEH-yeh.

Grammar Focus — Impersonal Weather Sentences

In Russian, weather expressions use no subject — just an adverb or short adjective. There is no "it" equivalent.

RussianPronunciationEnglish
Холодно.HOH-lud-nuh.It is cold.
Тепло.tep-LOH.It is warm.
Солнечно.SOHL-neech-nuh.It is sunny.
Облачно.OH-bluch-nuh.It is cloudy.
Ветрено.VYEH-tree-nuh.It is windy.
Идёт снег.ee-DYOT snyek.It is snowing. (lit. "snow goes")
Идёт дождь.ee-DYOT dohshch'.It is raining. (lit. "rain goes")
Note: Идёт снег / дождь literally means "snow/rain goes" — Russian uses to go for precipitation. From the verb идти (eed-TEE) = to go (on foot). Same verb pattern as Verbs of Motion (QuickStudy p. 5).

Выходные — The Weekend

10 min
Teacher: Today is Sunday — воскресенье. These phrases let Mike talk about his weekend. Introduce perfective vs. imperfective gently through the examples below.
#EnglishRussianPronunciation
6 This weekend I stayed home and read a book. На этих выходных я остался дома и читал книгу. Na EH-teekh vy-HOD-nyh ya as-TAL-sya DOH-mah ee chee-TAL KNEE-goo.
7 I finished the book by Saturday evening. К субботнему вечеру я дочитал книгу. K soob-BOT-nye-moo VYEH-chee-roo ya da-chee-TAL KNEE-goo.
8 On Sundays I usually have a coffee and go for a walk. По воскресеньям я обычно пью кофе и гуляю. Pa vas-kree-SYEN'-yam ya a-BYCH-nuh p'yoo KOH-feh ee goo-LYA-yoo.
9 Next week I have a lot of work to do. На следующей неделе у меня много работы. Na SLYED-oo-yoo-shchey nee-DYEH-leh oo mee-NYA MNOH-guh ra-BOH-ty.
10 But today I am resting and learning Russian! Но сегодня я отдыхаю и учу русский! No see-VOHD-nyah ya at-dy-HA-yoo ee oo-CHOO ROOS-kiy!

Grammar Focus — Imperfective vs. Perfective Aspect (Вид глагола)

Every Russian verb has two forms — this is the core concept of the verb system (QuickStudy p. 4, Aspect).
Imperfective (несовершенный вид)
читал
chee-TAL
→ was reading, kept reading
ongoing, repeated, unfinished
Perfective (совершенный вид)
дочитал
da-chee-TAL
→ finished reading, read it through
completed, whole event
Sentences 6 vs. 7: читал книгу = was reading (ongoing activity). дочитал книгу = finished the book (completed). The prefix до- adds the meaning of "completion." Many perfectives are formed this way — by adding a prefix to an imperfective verb.

Личные фразы — Personal Phrases

8 min
Teacher: Adapt these to Mike's actual life. Replace details as needed — these are his sentences to own. Let him suggest edits and build his own versions.
#EnglishRussianPronunciation
11 I am Canadian, but I am learning Russian. Я канадец, но я учу русский язык. Ya kah-NAH-deets, no ya oo-CHOO ROOS-kiy ya-ZYK.
12 I have been studying Russian for a few weeks already. Я учу русский уже несколько недель. Ya oo-CHOO ROOS-kiy oo-ZHEH NYEHS-kol'-kuh nee-DYEL'.
13 Russian is difficult, but very interesting. Русский трудный, но очень интересный. ROOS-kiy TROOD-nyy, no OH-chen' een-tee-RYEHS-nyy.
14 I find the grammar especially challenging. Грамматика мне особенно трудна. Gra-MAH-tee-kah mnye a-SOH-been-nuh trood-NAH.
15 My teacher is very patient and explains everything clearly. Мой учитель очень терпелив и объясняет всё ясно. Moy oo-CHEE-tyel' OH-chen' teer-pee-LEEV ee ab-yas-NYA-yet fsyo YAHS-nuh.
Notice — Short-form Adjective: In sentence 14, трудна (trood-NAH) is the short form of трудный, used as a predicate. Compare: трудная задача (a difficult task — long form, attribute) vs. задача трудна (the task is difficult — short form, predicate). See QuickStudy p. 4, Short Form of the Adjective.
