01

Warm-Up — The Russian Alphabet & Key Sounds

5 min
Teacher: Mike can already read Cyrillic. Use these 5 minutes to review the sounds he'll need most in today's lesson. Focus on letters that trip up English speakers.
LetterSounds LikeMemory Trick for MikeExample
Е / е yeh Like "ye" in "yes" неделя (week)
Ё / ё yoh Like "yo" in "yoga" — always stressed её (her)
Ы / ы ɨ Clench your jaw, say "ee" — sounds murky. Like "i" in "ill" said with pursed lips мы (we), вы (you pl.)
Р / р r (rolled) Tap/trill like a soft Spanish R — not English R среда (Wednesday)
Ж / ж zh Like the "s" in "measure" or "treasure" воскресенье (Sunday)
Tip for Mike: Russian vowels reduce when unstressed. The letter о sounds like "ah" when not stressed — so воскресенье sounds like vuh-skree-SYEN-yeh, not "voh-SKRYE-SEN-ye".
02

Дни недели — Days of the Week

10 min
Teacher: Point out that today (26 Apr 2026) is Sunday = воскресенье. Russian week starts on Monday. Days are not capitalised (unlike English or French). Draw attention to the -к pattern in the middle of the week and the Slavic roots.
#RussianPronunciationEnglishMemory Hook
1понедельник puh-nee-DYEL-neek Monday From "после недели" — after the week
2вторник FTOR-neek Tuesday второй = second; "B" → "V" sound!
3среда sree-DAH Wednesday середина = middle — mid-week!
4четверг chet-VYERK Thursday четыре = four
5пятница PYAT-nee-tsuh Friday пять = five
6суббота soo-BOH-tuh Saturday Same root as "Sabbath"
7воскресенье vuh-skree-SYEN-yeh Sunday ← Today! воскресение = resurrection (Easter roots)
Key phrase: Сегодня (see-VOHD-nyuh) = "Today"  |  Сегодня воскресенье. = "Today is Sunday."

📋 Quick Drill — Teacher asks, Mike answers

  1. Point to Thursday on a calendar and ask: "Это вторник?" (Is this Tuesday?) — Mike should say Нет, это четверг.
  2. Ask: "Какой сегодня день?" (What day is today?) — Expected: Сегодня воскресенье.
  3. Ask Mike to spell out среда aloud letter by letter.
  4. Say the day in Russian; Mike translates to English. Do all 7.

Pronunciation note: The soft sign ь at the end of пять, шесть, семь, etc. softens the preceding consonant — hold the tongue against the roof of the mouth briefly before releasing. English has no exact equivalent; think of the "t" in "ature" (nat-ure).

📋 Quick Drill

  • Count 1–10 aloud together, then Mike counts alone.
  • Teacher says a number in English; Mike responds in Russian.
  • Connection game: "пять" → what day? пятница!
  • Write today's date: 26 апреля — "двадцать шесть" (twenty-six). Sounds like: dvah-TSUT' shest'. Just for fun — don't drill yet.
04

Личные местоимения — Personal Pronouns

10 min
Teacher: Introduce only the nominative (subject) pronouns today. The QuickStudy guide (Page 2, Personal Pronouns table) shows all six cases — reassure Mike that for now we only need the top row. Note: Russian has no verb "to be" in the present tense — "I am a student" = literally "I student."
RussianPronunciationEnglishNotes for Mike
я yah I Always lowercase, even at start — wait, actually always lowercase mid-sentence. Capitalised only at sentence start.
ты tih you (singular, informal) Use with friends, family, children.
он ohn he / it (masc. noun) Also used for masculine nouns
она ah-NAH she / it (fem. noun) Also used for feminine nouns
мы mih we The ы sound — jaw clenched "ee"
вы vih you (plural OR formal sing.) Always capitalised Вы when formal/polite.
они ah-NEE they Looks like "they" written backwards almost!
The "Zero Copula" Rule: In the present tense, Russian drops the verb "to be."
Я студент. = Yah stoo-DYENT. = "I am a student." — "I student."
Она канадка. = Ah-NAH kah-NAHT-kuh. = "She is Canadian." (female)
This is one of the most important patterns Mike will keep using!

📋 Fill in the Pronoun

  1. ___ канадец. (Mike, about himself) → Я канадец. (I am Canadian.)
  2. ___ студент? (asking Mike) → Ты студент? (Are you a student?)
  3. Это Анна. ___ русская. → Она русская. (She is Russian.)
  4. ___ учим русский. → Мы учим русский. (We are learning Russian.)
05

Мини-диалог — Mini Dialogue & Wrap-Up

7 min
Teacher: Role-play this short dialogue. Teacher plays Анна; Mike plays himself. All vocabulary has been covered in today's lesson. Run it twice — once with prompts, once without.
Анна:
Привет, Майк! Как дела?
Pree-VYET, Mayk! Kak dee-LAH?
Hi, Mike! How are you?
Майк:
Привет! Хорошо, спасибо. А ты?
Pree-VYET! Hah-rah-SHOH, spuh-SEE-buh. Ah tih?
Hi! Good, thanks. And you?
Анна:
Тоже хорошо! Какой сегодня день?
TOH-zheh hah-rah-SHOH! Kah-KOY see-VOHD-nyuh dyen'?
Good too! What day is today?
Майк:
Сегодня воскресенье.
See-VOHD-nyuh vuh-skree-SYEN-yeh.
Today is Sunday.
Анна:
Отлично! Ты канадец?
At-LEECH-nuh! Tih kah-NAH-deets?
Excellent! Are you Canadian?
Майк:
Да, я канадец. Я учу русский.
Dah, yah kah-NAH-deets. Yah oo-CHOO ROOS-kiy.
Yes, I am Canadian. I am learning Russian.
Анна:
Молодец! До свидания!
Muh-lah-DYETS! Duh svee-DAH-nee-yuh!
Well done! Goodbye!
Майк:
До свидания!
Duh svee-DAH-nee-yuh!
Goodbye!

📚 Homework for Mike (before next lesson)

  1. Memorise all 7 days of the week — say them aloud every morning.
  2. Count to 10 in Russian, then count backwards from 10 to 1.
  3. Write a 3-sentence "diary entry" in Russian using today's template:
    Сегодня [день]. Я [profession/name]. Я учу русский.
  4. Find the days of the week on a Russian calendar app — notice they start on Monday!
