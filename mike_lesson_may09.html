The Ы Sound — Deeper Practice

10 min
Teacher: Last week we introduced ы. This week we make it a reflex. The trick: smile slightly, pull your tongue back, like saying "oo" with the lips of "ee." Have Mike put a finger on his jaw to feel the position.
Ы ы
"ɨ" — the Russian back vowel
Tongue back, lips relaxed, no smile
Quick test: Say "ee" — your tongue is forward, lips wide. Now say "oo" — your tongue is back, lips rounded. For ы, keep the tongue back like "oo" but the lips spread like "ee." It feels weird. That means you're doing it right.

Easily Confused Words — The "False Friends" of Russian

10 min
Teacher: These pairs trip up every English speaker because they look or sound similar. For each pair, say both words, point at the page, and ask Mike to translate. The goal is recognition, not memorisation.
⏰ Time words
вчера vchee-RA yesterday
vs
вечером VYE-chee-rum in the evening
Both start with вчер-/вечер- but mean different things. Вчера = yesterday (past day). Вечером = in the evening (time of day). Tip: вечер = evening (cf. "vespers"); вчера stresses the last syllable.
👤 Pronoun cases (I / me)
меня mee-NYA me (acc./gen.)
vs
мне mnye to me (dat.)
Both come from я (I). Меня = direct object ("he sees me") or possession ("I have" = literally "by me"). Мне = "to/for me" — when something is given, told, or felt by me.
🎯 Pronoun cases (you / her)
тебя tee-BYA you (acc.)
vs
тебе tee-BYE to you (dat.)
Same pattern as меня/мне. Тебя = "you" as object. Тебе = "to you" — same -е ending as мне. Question: Как тебя зовут? = "What's your name?" (lit. "How do they call you?")
🌅 Time of day
утром OOT-rum in the morning
vs
завтра ZAHF-truh tomorrow
These confuse many learners because завтрак = breakfast (morning meal). But завтра alone = tomorrow! Утром = in the morning (any morning). Завтра утром = "tomorrow morning."
📍 "Here" words
здесь zdyes' here (location)
vs
сюда syu-DA to here (motion)
Russian splits "here" into position and direction. Здесь = "I am here." Сюда = "Come here." (motion toward) Same with там/туда (there/to there).
🏠 "At home" / "to home"
дома DOH-ma at home
vs
домой da-MOY homeward
Same root дом (house/home). Дома = "I am at home" (location). Домой = "I'm going home" (motion). Я иду домой NOT Я иду дома.
The Big Pattern: Russian distinguishes where you ARE from where you're GOING. English uses "home" or "here" for both — Russian uses different words. This will keep coming back as we learn cases. Don't memorise; absorb through use.

Mabo is male! Russian has two words for "dog": собака is grammatically feminine regardless of the dog's actual sex (you'd still say моя собака for Mabo). пёс is the masculine word — use it when you want to emphasise he's a male dog: мой пёс Мабо = "my dog Mabo."

Sentences for Humans — Talking About the Dog

#EnglishRussianPronunciation
11 I'm going to walk the dog. Я иду гулять с собакой. Ya ee-DOO goo-LYAT' s sa-BA-koy.
12 Do you want to go for a walk? Хочешь гулять? HOH-cheesh' goo-LYAT'?
13 In the morning we go to the park. Утром мы идём в парк. OOT-rum my ee-DYOM v park.
14 Yesterday evening we walked for an hour. Вчера вечером мы гуляли час. Vchee-RA VYE-chee-rum my goo-LYA-lee chas.
15 Give me the leash, please. Дай мне поводок, пожалуйста. Day mnye pa-va-DOK, pa-ZHA-loo-sta.
16 My dog loves the park. Мой пёс любит парк. Moy pyos LYU-beet park.
17 It's time to go home. Пора домой. Pa-RA da-MOY.
18 He's tired. Let's rest. Он устал. Давай отдохнём. On oos-TAL. Da-VAY at-dah-NYOM.
Confused-words spotting: Sentence 14 has вчера AND вечером — yesterday in the evening. Sentence 15 uses мне ("to me"). Sentence 17 uses домой (motion: homeward), not дома. All from today's lesson!

📚 Домашнее задание — Homework for Mike

  1. Every time you walk the dog this week, say three commands in Russian: Пошли! Сидеть! Ко мне! Out loud. The dog won't judge.
